Ramadan Countdown

1. What is Ramadan?

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar. Muslims fast from dawn until sunset. It is a month of worship, patience, and self-discipline.

2. Why Ramadan is Important

Ramadan is important because the Qur'an was first revealed during this month. Muslims use this time to increase their worship and become closer to Allah.

3. Purpose of Fasting

Fasting teaches patience, self-control, and gratitude. It also reminds people to help those who are less fortunate.

4. The Last 10 Nights

The last ten nights of Ramadan are the most powerful nights for prayer and seeking forgiveness.

Laylatul Qadr

Laylatul Qadr (Night of Power) is better than a thousand months. Muslims pray deeply and make many duas during these nights.

Qur'an Reminder

"The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months." — Qur'an 97:3

Duas for Ramadan and the Last 10 Nights

1. Allahumma innaka afuwwun tuhibbul afwa fa'fu anni

O Allah, You are Most Forgiving and love forgiveness, so forgive me.

2. Rabbana atina fid dunya hasanah wa fil akhirati hasanah wa qina adhaban nar

Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter and protect us from the punishment of the Fire.

3. Allahumma inni as'aluka al-jannah

O Allah, I ask You for Paradise.

4. Rabbi zidni ilma

My Lord, increase me in knowledge.

5. Allahumma inni as'aluka al-afiyah

O Allah, I ask You for health and protection.

Eid al-Fitr

After Ramadan ends, Muslims celebrate Eid al-Fitr with prayer, charity, and family gatherings.
